# Hanami

> **"Every visitor should feel expected."**

Hanami is the digital experience of being intentionally welcomed. It is an Emotional Operating System designed for products that require trust, warmth, and humanity. It is the opposite of a sterile SaaS dashboard; it is a meticulously crafted space that respects the user's time and attention.

---

## The Mood

### Architecture
- **Tea Houses:** Intimate scale, low ceilings, natural framing.
- **Courtyards:** A private space within a public world; safe and enclosed.
- **Verandas (Engawa):** The blurred boundary between inside and outside. Elements shouldn't feel boxed in by hard borders.
- **Gardens:** Meticulously curated but meant to look entirely natural. Nothing is accidental.

### Nature
- **Morning Fog:** Softness, diffused light, obscured sharp edges.
- **Wind through Bamboo:** Gentle, organic, unpredictable movement.
- **Moss after rain:** Deep, rich, saturated but muted colors.
- **Paper Lanterns:** Warm, radiating glows.

### Materials
- **Linen & Cotton:** Soft, breathable, tactile backgrounds.
- **Handmade Paper:** Subtle grain, imperfect but beautiful edges.
- **Clay:** Earthy, grounded elements.
- **Wood:** Warm, organic structural framing.

### Photography
- Soft, diffused morning light.
- Warm, inviting interiors.
- Deep, soft shadows that ground objects rather than float them.
- Empty space that lets the subject breathe.

### Music
- **Acoustic piano or cello:** Slow pacing, letting the notes ring out.
- **Silence between notes:** The space between interactions is as important as the interactions themselves.
- **Tempo:** Adagio. Never rushed.

### Films
- *Ozu films:* Still camera, low angles, focusing on human quietness.
- *Studio Ghibli:* The reverence for small, mundane moments (pouring tea, sunlight hitting dust).

## Behavioral Rules

1. **How it moves:** Like water or breathing. Easing curves should be elongated, smooth, and decelerating. Nothing snaps into place. Elements fade and drift in.
2. **How it breathes:** Wide margins. Unrushed vertical rhythm. Text line heights are slightly taller than normal. It asks the user to slow down and read.
3. **How it welcomes:** Interactions shouldn't demand attention; they should gently acknowledge it. Hovering over a card doesn't throw a harsh shadow; it gently warms the background. 

## The Memorable Moment (Signature Interaction)
**The Welcome Effect**

The interface quietly makes room for the visitor. Elements don't animate toward the cursor—they acknowledge the user's presence through subtle changes in spacing, warmth, focus, and rhythm. 

When your cursor enters a section, the layout subtly rearranges itself:
* The heading gains slightly more breathing room (spacing expands).
* Distracting surrounding elements fade or lose visual noise.
* Background surfaces warm slightly.
* The page quietly says, "I noticed you."

It reacts to *presence*, not just to the *cursor*. It changes how the user feels (welcomed, calm) rather than just what the user sees.
